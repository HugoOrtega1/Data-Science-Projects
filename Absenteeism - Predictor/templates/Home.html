Absenteeism

The Absenteeism Web app is an application that predicts whether or not an employee will be excessively absent from work, using Machine Learning.The codes for this project can be found on my Githhub repo. The Machine learning model used is based on a database created with records of absenteeism at work from July 2007 to July 2010 at a courier company in Brazil.

Dashboard

The dashboards below were created by using Tableau after I pre-processed the absenteeism dataset. An analysis of the data gives enough information to come to a couple of theories and surprising conclusions. In the bar chart, children vs. absenteeism time in hours, employees with two children or more were more likely to be absent from work. Observing the scatter plot related to Distance to work versus absenteeism time in hours visualizes that distance has no relation to absenteeism, neither does the average workload. The age vs. absenteeism time hours signifies that in average, employees in their late twenties to mid-thirties and employees in their fifties had a high chance of absenteeism. A theory that would need further analysis is that it may be due to the quarter and midlife crisis.
